Uncharted Artists

Naseeruddin Shah
Naseeruddin Shah:

An acclaimed actor, director, and one of the founding members of the influential theatre group "Motley Productions," Naseeruddin Shah has made significant contributions to Indian and international cinema and theatre.

Shabana Azmi
Shabana Azmi:

A versatile actress known for her work in films, Shabana Azmi has also been actively involved in theatre. She has been associated with the IPTA (Indian People's Theatre Association) and has performed in numerous stage productions.

Amrish Puri
Amrish Puri:

Although best known for his work in films, Amrish Puri was also a respected theatre actor. He was associated with the Prithvi Theatre in Mumbai and had a notable career on stage.

Nawazuddin Siddiqui
Nawazuddin Siddiqui:

While achieving fame in Bollywood, Nawazuddin Siddiqui has a strong background in theatre. He has worked with theatre groups like Barry John's group and has been appreciated for his stage performances.

Daroji Eramma
Daroji Eramma:

Daroji Eramma, popularly known as Burrakatha Eeramma was a folk singer and performer of the Burrakatha, a folk art form of epic storytelling from South India. She was awarded several awards including the Rajyotsava Prashasti in 1999.

Chittani Ramachandr
Chittani Ramachandr:

Chittani Ramachandra Hegde is the first Yakshagana artist to win the prestigious Padmashri award.He started his Yakshagana career when he was just 7 years old. He started performing in main roles when he was just 14 years old and performed on stage until a few days preceding his death.

Bhikhari Thakur
Bhikhari Thakur:

Bhikhari Thakur from Bihar is famous for his contributions to the art form, particularly for the creation of the Bidesia genre. His work revolutionized Indian theatre, blending cultural narratives and vibrant performances, leaving an enduring legacy celebrated for its profound impact.

Ratan Thiyam
Ratan Thiyam:

Ratan Thiyam is known for his work in reviving traditional Manipuri theatre and his innovative contributions to contemporary Indian theatre. Renowned for powerful and experimental productions, he revitalized Indian theatre, earning national and international acclaim.

Some Unknown facts

Fact:

Gubbi Veeranna, a prominent theater personality in Karnataka, played a significant role in bringing Yakshagana to the urban audience. He adapted Yakshagana stories into the Kannada language, making it more accessible to a broader audience.

Fact:

Recognizing its cultural significance, Yakshagana was designated as one of the intangible cultural heritage elements by UNESCO in 2008. Efforts are being made to preserve and promote this traditional art form for future generations.

Fact:

Ancient Indian theater produced classical Sanskrit dramas, with notable playwrights like Kalidasa contributing to the genre. Works like "Shakuntala" are considered masterpieces of classical Sanskrit drama.

Fact:

Theatre was extremely critical of the British Raj.Neeldarpan', a Bengali play, in 1860, sparked off this phase. Theatre mobilised thousands to join the freedom struggle.

Fact:

Kutiyattam, a traditional Sanskrit theater form from Kerala, has been recognized by UNESCO as a Masterpiece of the Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity. It is one of the oldest surviving forms of theater.

Fact:

The roots of Indian theatrical arts can be traced back to ancient times, with mentions of drama and performance found in ancient texts like the Natya Shastra, believed to have been written by Sage Bharata around the 2nd century BCE.

Fact:

Theatre in India began in Calcutta and Bombay in the second half of the 18th century.

Fact:

It was from the second half of the nineteenth century that the socio-economic conditions of Bengal and the essence of nationalism were reflected in the plays.
